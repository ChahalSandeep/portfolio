About Me

I’m a builder of intelligent systems with 8+ years of cross-functional experience in AI, machine learning, computer vision, and applied data science. I specialize in taking ideas from experimentation to deployment — whether it’s model evaluation, fraud analytics, robotics, computer vision, or internal AI tooling. My work consistently balances technical rigor with business impact, helping teams move faster while solving the right problems.

My experience includes leading impactful projects at companies like Sift Science and Stats Perform. At Sift, I developed fraud detection models, cross-sell optimization strategies, and internal LLM-powered assistants. At Stats Perform, I contributed to video-based player tracking, court segmentation, and anomaly event detection, building classification pipelines and video-based OCR systems. Across both roles, I’ve developed model evaluation frameworks, scalable ML pipelines, and testing infrastructure to translate research into high-impact production systems.

I hold a Master’s in Computer Science from the University of Texas at Arlington, where my research focused on adaptive motion planning using RRT, potential fields, and directional nearest neighbors. During my academic journey, I worked hands-on with robots like the APM Rover and LEGO Mindstorms, integrating GPS, IMU, and sonar sensors for intelligent navigation and control. My internships included improving robotic testing uptime at Fetch Robotics using CV-based automation, and developing localization algorithms at SignalAware using Bluetooth and Wi-Fi.

I bring a systems-thinking mindset to everything from data ingestion to deployment and thrive in environments where research, experimentation, and engineering come together to solve real-world problems. I enjoy designing scalable ML pipelines, creating robust evaluation frameworks, and collaborating across product, data, and engineering teams to drive measurable impact. Passionate about building AI that learns, adapts, and scales, I work at the intersection of applied research and product-driven engineering.

When I’m not coding or building models, you’ll find me exploring things completely outside of AI and my comfort zone to stay sharp, curious, and ready to embrace new challenges.
